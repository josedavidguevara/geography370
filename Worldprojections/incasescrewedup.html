Homework 3: In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

Describe in your own words how you displayed the map in different projections using QGIS

I started by downloading the shapefile of the globe's country, and used the indicatrix plug in to see the distortions the default projection had (4326). Then, using the feature on the bottom right, I changed the project CRS to different projections, which is known as reprojecting on the fly. The indicatrix allowed me to observe the different ways different projections distort the map

WGS84 Projection

The shape and size of countries seem to be pretty well preserved, especially towards the center.

Aitoff Projection

This projection looks more like an oval, and it seems less two-dimensional that the previous one. The lines are curved on this one.

Pseudo Mercator

This is the projection I am most familiar with. However, I can now tell how dramatic the size distortion is when it comes to countries like greenland.

Winkel I

This projection also has a globe-ish shape, like the Aitoff, but this one is more elongated and the indicator dots are on straighter lines

Equal Area Cylindrical

This projection distorts shape, especially at the poles, but it preserves area. This might be useful when you need to know the size of countries.

Equidistant Conic

This projection preserves distances along the meridian but distorts shape and size

Azimuthal Equidistant

This projection looks like the UN logo. It is centered around the north pole, and preserves distance and direction from the center"

Natural Earth II

This map compromises all aspects in order to reduce overall distortion.

Orthographic (View from Space)

This projection looks most like a globe, while you can't see the entire world (it gets distorted in the ends) it is a good representation of one side of the planet.
